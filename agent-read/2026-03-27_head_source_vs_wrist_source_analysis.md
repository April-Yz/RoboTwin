## 6. What the actual length difference tells us

Sampling several `y` episodes gives:

```text
id=0
head_plan   263 frames @ 10 fps
zed_replay   39 frames @ 5 fps
left_wrist   39 frames @ 5 fps
right_wrist  39 frames @ 5 fps

id=8
head_plan   259 frames @ 10 fps
zed_replay   41 frames @ 5 fps
left_wrist   41 frames @ 5 fps
right_wrist  41 frames @ 5 fps

id=10
head_plan   263 frames @ 10 fps
zed_replay   35 frames @ 5 fps
left_wrist   35 frames @ 5 fps
right_wrist  35 frames @ 5 fps
```

This shows:

### Planner head and retarget wrist are not the same temporal sampling stream

- `head_cam_plan.mp4`:
  - from planner
  - many more frames, usually `255~263`
  - fps is `10`
- `left/right_wrist_replay.mp4`:
  - from hand retarget
  - far fewer frames, usually `35~60`
  - fps is `5`

So they differ not only in source pipeline, but also in:
- fps
- frame count
- temporal sampling strategy

---

## 7. Why planner head is much longer than retarget replay

Based on the observed behavior, planner `head_cam_plan.mp4` is not simply one frame per retarget source frame.

It appears to include:
- interpolated execution motion
- multiple stages (`pregrasp / grasp / action`)
- intermediate execution frames
- extra settle / hold frames

Meanwhile, the retarget outputs:
- `zed_replay.mp4`
- `left_wrist_replay.mp4`
- `right_wrist_replay.mp4`

are closer to a replay driven by the retarget input sequence itself, so their lengths remain close to `world_targets_and_status.npz`.

Therefore:
- planner head is a **dense execution video**
- retarget wrist is a **sparser supervision/replay video**

It is therefore expected that their frame counts do not match.

---

## 8. What this implies for the current pi0 processing step

The current pipeline is effectively mixing two different sources:

### Visual head
- repaint result based on planner head cam

### Visual wrist + control pose
- wrist replays + world targets from the hand-retarget pipeline

Therefore, when `process_repainted_headcam_with_wrist.py` does:

```text
usable_len = min(T_pose, T_head, T_left, T_right)
```

`T_head` is almost always much larger than the other three.

So the final preserved length is still determined by:
- `T_pose`
- `T_left`
- `T_right`

not by planner head.

---

## 9. Most important takeaway

### What is consistent
Inside the hand-retarget directory:
- `zed_replay`
- `left_wrist_replay`
- `right_wrist_replay`
- `world_targets_and_status.npz`

are basically aligned in length.

### What is not consistent
The head video used for repaint in this pipeline is:
- not `hand_detections_<id>/zed_replay.mp4`
- but planner-side `head_cam_plan.mp4`

So the current mismatch is not an anomaly. It happens because:
- **the upstream head and the downstream wrist are not from the same source stream**
